About Me

My name is Jack Corp, and I'm a historian from the Ozarks. My research focuses on models of holiness, sanctity, and the supernatural in rural communities, framed through the heuristics of hegemony and counterpower, and analyzed from a methodologically anti-state perspective.

I'm currently a Master's student in History at the University of British Columbia.

My Research

  1. THEMES across my studies:
    • Religion, cosmology, & rural social organizaiton
    • Slavery & the body
    • Imperialism & political economy in early state-systems
    • Hegemony & subaltern counterpower
    • Philosophies of History & construction of historical compositions

  2. SCOPE of my work:
    • Late Antiquity & Early Middle Ages
    • Carolingian Francia
    • US South & the Ozarks

  3. HISTORIOGRAPHICAL and theoretical interests:
      Anarchist critiques, Confucian theories of history, global and comparative approaches, historicity and memory, history as critique
